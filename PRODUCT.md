# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Women across all life stages — cycle tracking, fertility, pregnancy, menopause, and adolescence — each using the module relevant to where they are right now. They open the app when something feels off, when they want to log after an event, or as a daily check-in habit. Their state of mind ranges from curious to anxious to quietly hopeful. They are not medical professionals — they want clarity, not clinical data.

## Product Purpose

MyStree Soul helps women understand and feel in control of their bodies at every life stage. The app provides cycle tracking, mood and symptom logging, AI-powered insights, nourishment guidance, and a companion character (Bloop) that makes the experience feel personal and safe. Success is a user saying "I'm in control of my health" after their first week — not "this app has a lot of features."

## Brand Personality

Intimate and companion-like. Bloop, the AI character, is the emotional heart of the experience. The app should feel like a best friend who happens to know everything about your body — warm, non-judgmental, a little playful, deeply trustworthy. Three words: warm, knowing, gentle.

## Anti-references

Corporate health tech — no navy blue, formal sans-serif typography, or layouts that look like a hospital portal or enterprise dashboard. The app must never feel cold, bureaucratic, or intimidating. Avoid the aesthetic of insurance portals, NHS-style health trackers, or anything that signals "medical form."

## Design Principles

1. **Bloop is the anchor** — Every screen that carries emotional weight should have Bloop's presence. The companion character creates warmth and reduces clinical coldness. When in doubt, add Bloop.

2. **Empowerment, not anxiety** — Every data point, insight, and notification should leave the user feeling more in control, never more worried. Frame information as understanding, not warning.

3. **One life stage at a time** — Meet users exactly where they are. Don't surface pregnancy content to someone tracking their cycle. The experience adapts to the user's life phase and does not overwhelm with irrelevant modules.

4. **Warmth before precision** — The interface should feel warm and personal before it feels clinically precise. A slightly rounded corner, a terracotta accent, a floating Bloop badge — these matter more than pixel-perfect data visualisation.

5. **Quiet confidence** — Never loud, never urgent, never pushy. The UI is calm and assured. Notifications are gentle. CTAs don't shout. The app trusts the user to engage on her own terms.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard best practices for now — WCAG AA contrast ratios, minimum 44px touch targets, readable font sizes. Full accessibility pass is planned for a later milestone.
